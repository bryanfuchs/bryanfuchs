Brake Calipers

The brake calipers operate by having pistons squeeze on both sides of a brake rotor, contacted by brake pads sitting on the caliper’s surface. They are a custom designed fixed caliper.

Suspension Uprights

The front upright is the interface between the spinning components of the wheel and the links and frame of the car. It connects the links and driveshaft from the suspension arms to the hub and wheel assembly. On top of that, it carries brake components - the caliper bracket and rotor. These were custom designed to incorporate spherical bearings.

About Me

Howdy, my name is Bryan Fuchs and I’m a Junior studying Mechanical Engineering at Cornell University. I was born in Singapore but spent my teenage years in Austin, Texas. I am especially interested in automotives, rapid prototyping, product testing, and the intersection of applying aesthetics to engineering.


Through the years of academia and exploring different projects, I have found that I learn and excel best in hands on applications, and constantly seek to apply subject fundamentals to extracurricular projects. I love to collaborate with my peers in hopes of achieving the best possible result, and a creative problems are some of my favorite problems to attempt.


The purpose of this website is to show people the projects I’ve spent countless hours perfecting and manufacturing. Hopefully, I am able to frame some of the skills I’ve picked up over the past few years, as well as document my passions and interests to share with friends and family.
